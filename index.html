Tetris

The most simple game on our list but perhaps, the most impactful one. Created by Soviet software engineer Alexey Pajitnov in 1984 for the Electronika 60 computer, Tetris has since sold over 202 million copies – approximately 70 million physical units and 132 million paid mobile game downloads, and ported over 65 platforms setting a Guiness world record for the most ported video game title. The colourful tile-matching puzzle game deserves to be on top of our retro hits list.

X-COM Games

1994 marked the entry of the popular science fiction video game franchise X-COM. Developed by Mythos Games and MicroProse as a Strategy game, it experienced various genre-changes throughout and was later adapted and published under Firaxis games and 2K games respectively. Whether it be a space combat flight simulator or engaging in underwater combat with extra-terrestrial beings, X-COM and it’s spinoff games have become fan-favourites, with “UFO: Enemy Unknown” making it to several best games of all time lists. These games are a must for retro sci-fi lovers!

Metal Gear Games

There is no chance you haven’t come across Hideo Kojima’s “Metal Gear” if you are passionate about videogames. This action-adventure series often follows around Snake or Big Boss, a special forces operative who is assigned the task of finding the titular superweapon "Metal Gear", a bipedal walking tank with the ability to launch nuclear weapons. Dune Videogame Series

Following the story of the award winning 1965 novel “Dune” by Frank Herbert and later adapted for a movie, Cryo Interactive/Virgin Interactive managed to make a videogame that beautifully blends adventure with strategy which was an instant hit with sales of 20,000 units in its first week alone. It’s successor, Dune II, is often considered the first "mainstream modern real-time strategy game". NeoWave highly recommends checking out this awesome series, if you haven’t already.
